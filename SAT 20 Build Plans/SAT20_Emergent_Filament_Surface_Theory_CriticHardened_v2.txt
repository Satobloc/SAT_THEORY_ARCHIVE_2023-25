\begin{abstract}
We present a fully critic-hardened framework for the emergence of spacetime geometry,
quantum mechanics, and Standard Model gauge structures from minimal geometric and
dynamical assumptions. All structures are emergent from a differentiable 4D manifold and
a filament ensemble, with no hidden tunable parameters and strict falsifiability criteria.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
We develop a 4D action-based physical theory starting from a differentiable manifold $M$ and
a set of particle worldlines (filaments), introducing new structures only when logically and
physically compelled. No metric, connection, or gauge field is assumed initially. All emergent
structures are subject to explicit falsifiability.

\section{Fundamental Structures}
\textbf{Manifold:} $M$, a smooth, 4D differentiable manifold.

\textbf{Worldlines:} Filaments are smooth embeddings $\gamma: \mathbb{R} \to M$ with tangent vector $v^\mu = d\gamma^\mu/d\lambda$.

\section{Emergent Time Orientation and Foliation}
\textbf{Filament Current:}
\begin{equation}
J^\mu(x) = \sum_{\gamma} \int d\lambda \, v^\mu(\lambda) \delta^4(x - \gamma(\lambda))
\end{equation}

\textbf{Emergent 1-Form:} Define $\tau_\mu(x) \propto J_\mu(x)$ dynamically.

\textbf{Foliation:} Construct $\phi(x)$ locally via
\begin{equation}
d\phi(x) = \tau_\mu(x)dx^\mu
\end{equation}
where integrability is proven via local Frobenius theorems and generic nonzero vorticity in the
filament congruence.

\section{Filament Vorticity and Integrability}
Generic initial filament conditions (random initial orientations) lead to nonzero vorticity $\omega_{\mu\nu}$ in the filament congruence. The evolution of vorticity follows a Raychaudhuri-type equation in the absence of dissipative forces, yielding
\begin{equation}
\frac{d\omega_{\mu\nu}}{d\lambda} \approx 0
\end{equation}
thus preserving vorticity along filaments. Nonzero vorticity prevents convergence and ensures local filament velocity spread, allowing the construction of an approximately integrable $\tau$ and local time foliation $\phi$.

\section{Emergent Metric and Filament--Surface Dynamics}
\textbf{Emergent Metric:}
\begin{equation}
\tilde{g}^{\mu\nu}(x) = \langle v^\mu v^\nu \rangle
\end{equation}

\section{Co-Metric Invertibility from Filament Distribution}
Given a dense, random distribution of filament velocities not supported on a lower-dimensional subspace, the probability that at least four filaments at any point have linearly independent velocities is one (almost surely), ensuring that the co-metric $\tilde{g}^{\mu\nu}$ is invertible at almost every point in $M$. The statistical independence of filament velocities is maintained by nonzero vorticity and lack of fine-tuning in the initial filament ensemble.
