\section*{Abstract}
We conduct an extended and comprehensive audit of Module O4 --- the Cosmological Constant Derivation in the SAT\_O framework. Beyond the original audit, we systematically stress-test the theory under extreme, realistic, and arbitrary conditions, assess mathematical and physical consistency, and identify limits, breakdown points, and critical failures.

\section*{Scope}
Module under audit: \textbf{SAT\_O4 - Cosmological Constant Derivation}.

Focus: Validate definitions, derivations, dimensional analysis, quantum consistency, falsifiability, and perform exhaustive stress-testing.

\section*{Methods}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Full review of equations:
    \[
    \ell_f = \left( \frac{2A}{T} \right)^{1/3}, \quad \Lambda_{\text{induced}} = \frac{1}{32\pi^2 \ell_f^4}
    \]
    \item Units and dimensional analysis verification.
    \item Parameter sweeps over 20 orders of magnitude.
    \item Real-world data insertion and validation.
    \item Edge case exploration: \( T \to 0 \), \( A \to 0 \), \(\ell_f \to \infty\).
    \item Higher-order correction analysis.
    \item Quantum consistency checks via effective \( \hbar_{\text{eff}} \).
\end{itemize}

\section*{Findings}

\subsection*{1. Realistic Physical Constants Test}
Using Planck-scale estimates:
\begin{align*}
\ell_f &\approx 3.72 \times 10^{-24} \, \text{m}, \\
\Lambda_{\text{induced}} &\approx 5.23 \times 10^{90} \, \text{m}^{-4}.
\end{align*}
Compared to observed \( \Lambda_{\text{obs}} \sim 10^{-52} \, \text{m}^{-2} \) squared, this is a mismatch by \( 10^{142} \) orders of magnitude, reproducing the cosmological constant problem.

\subsection*{3. Edge Case Exploration}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \(T \to 0\): \(\ell_f \to \infty\), \(\Lambda_{\text{induced}} \to 0\).
    \item \(A \to 0\): \(\ell_f \to 0\), \(\Lambda_{\text{induced}} \to \infty\) --- unphysical divergence.

\subsection*{4. Real-World Fitting Attempt}
Fitting \(\Lambda_{\text{obs}}\) yields:
\[
\ell_f \approx 1.18 \times 10^{25} \, \text{m}.
\]
Necessary tension:
\[
T \approx 1.22 \times 10^{-75} \, \text{N},
\]
or, fixing string tension:
\[
A \approx 8.2 \times 10^{95} \, \text{J}\cdot \text{m}.
\]
\textbf{Conclusion}: Parameters required are unphysical, either ultra-low tension or ultra-high rigidity.

\subsection*{5. Higher-Order Corrections}
Curvature corrections \( O(\ell_f^2 R^2) \) are suppressed for small \(\ell_f\), but for \(\ell_f \sim 10^{25}\,\text{m}\), corrections are negligible even near black holes.

\subsection*{6. Quantum Consistency}
Effective Planck constant scaling:
\[
\hbar_{\text{eff}} \sim T \ell_f^2.
\]
With above parameters:
\[
\hbar_{\text{eff}} \sim 1.7 \times 10^{-25} \, \text{J}\cdot \text{s},
\]
significantly larger than observed \(\hbar\), implying nonstandard quantum behavior.

\subsection*{7. Unphysical Scenarios}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Negative \(T\) or \(A\) results in complex \(\ell_f\) --- unphysical.
    \item Rapid spatial variation of \(T\) or \(A\) invalidates homogeneous \(\Lambda_{\text{induced}}\).
\end{itemize}

\section*{Conclusion}
While the SAT\_O4 module is mathematically coherent, achieving consistency with observed cosmology demands unphysical parameter choices. The model is highly falsifiable but struggles to meet empirical constraints without fine-tuning.
