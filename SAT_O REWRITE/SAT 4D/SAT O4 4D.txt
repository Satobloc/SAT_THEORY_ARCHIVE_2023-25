\section*{SAT\_O 2.0 Module O4: Predictive Falsifiability of Particle Stability}

\subsection*{Foundational Assumptions}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Filaments are real, 1D physical objects in 4D spacetime.
    \item Phase-locked bundles of filaments form hyperhelical structures.
    \item Topological stability in 4D constrains allowable stable filament interlocks.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Topological Stability Theorem}

\textbf{Theorem (Topological Stability of Hyperhelical Filament Bundles in 4D)}:  
Let $n$ denote the number of phase-locked filaments in a bundle. Define the stability function:
\[
\mathcal{S}(n) = 
\begin{cases}
1, & n = 2, 3, \\
0, & n \geq 4.
\end{cases}
\]
where:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $n = 2$ corresponds to a Hopf link (stable two-filament configuration).
    \item $n = 3$ corresponds to a Borromean link (stable three-filament configuration).
    \item $n \geq 4$ has no nontrivial stable interlock; such bundles are topologically unstable in 4D.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Particle Stability Predictions}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Mesons} (quark + anti-quark, $n=2$): Stable; long-lived.
    \item \textbf{Baryons} (three quarks, $n=3$): Stable; long-lived.
    \item \textbf{Tetraquarks} (four quark constituents, $n=4$): Unstable; transient resonance.
    \item \textbf{Pentaquarks} ($n=5$): Unstable; transient.
    \item \textbf{Hexaquarks} ($n=6$): Unstable; transient.
    \item \textbf{Higher n-body systems} ($n \geq 4$): Unstable; transient; rapid decay into lower-order composites.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Experimental Falsifiability}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Prediction}: Only 2-body (mesons) and 3-body (baryons) particle configurations are stable.
    \item \textbf{Exotic multiquark states} (tetraquarks, pentaquarks, etc.) must decay rapidly; no long-lived exotic hadrons exist.
    \item \textbf{Falsifiability}: Discovery of long-lived or stable tetraquarks, pentaquarks, or higher-order hadronic states would falsify the SAT\_O 2.0 4D Topological Model.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Concluding Remarks}

The SAT\_O 2.0 framework predicts a strict topological limit on stable particle composites, derived from 4D filament topology. Only mesons and baryons can exist as long-lived stable particles; exotics must decay transiently. This provides a sharp experimental test for the theory.
