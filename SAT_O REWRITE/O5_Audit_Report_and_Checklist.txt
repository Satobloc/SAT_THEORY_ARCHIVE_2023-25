\section*{Abstract}
This audit evaluates the derivation and properties of emergent gauge coupling constants from filament topological structures as prescribed in the SAT\_O framework. Testing includes dimensional analysis, stress testing under extreme parameter variations, symbolic limit evaluations, coupling ratio plausibility checks, quantum corrections commentary, and group-theoretical structure validation.

\section*{Scope}
Audit of the "Emergent Gauge Coupling Constants" module under the SAT\_O Ultra-Hostile Audit Kit guidelines.

\section*{Methods}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Explicit re-derivation of gauge coupling expressions.
    \item Dimensional consistency verification.
    \item Numerical stress testing with realistic and extreme parameter values.
    \item Symbolic limit analysis.
    \item Testing of coupling ratios against Standard Model values.
    \item Evaluation of quantum correction plausibility.
    \item Group-theoretical plausibility checks.
    \item Failure mode and falsifiability mapping.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Findings}

\subsection*{Gauge Coupling Formulas}
The gauge coupling constant is given by:
\[
g \sim \frac{1}{\sqrt{\rho_{\text{link}} \ell_f^2}}, \quad \ell_f = \left( \frac{2A}{T} \right)^{1/3}.
\]
Specific gauge groups:
\[
g_{U(1)} \sim \frac{1}{\sqrt{\rho_{\text{winding}} \ell_f^2}}, \quad g_{SU(2)} \sim \frac{1}{\sqrt{\rho_{\text{link}} \ell_f^2}}, \quad g_{SU(3)} \sim \frac{1}{\sqrt{\rho_{\text{triple link}} \ell_f^2}}.
\]

\subsection*{Stress Testing Results}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{High linking density:} Small gauge coupling, consistent with asymptotic freedom.
    \item \textbf{Low linking density:} Extremely large gauge coupling, nonphysical limit.
    \item \textbf{Zero density:} Singular behavior, infinite coupling.
    \item \textbf{Zero tension or rigidity:} Undefined filament scale, breakdown of model.
    \item \textbf{Small but nonzero parameters:} Stable but large gauge coupling values.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Symbolic Limit Analysis}
\[
\lim_{\rho \to 0^+} g = \infty, \quad \lim_{\rho \to \infty} g = 0,
\]
\[
\lim_{T \to 0^+} \ell_f = \infty, \quad \lim_{A \to 0^+} \ell_f = 0.
\]
These limits are physically reasonable and match expectations.

\subsection*{Coupling Ratio Predictions}
Standard Model values imply:
\[
\frac{g_{SU(2)}}{g_{U(1)}} \approx 1.81, \quad \frac{g_{SU(3)}}{g_{SU(2)}} \approx 1.88.
\]
Model predicts:
\[
\frac{g_{SU(2)}}{g_{U(1)}} \sim \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{\text{winding}}}{\rho_{\text{link}}}}, \quad \frac{g_{SU(3)}}{g_{SU(2)}} \sim \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{\text{link}}}{\rho_{\text{triple link}}}}.
\]
With \(\rho\) ratios of order 3, agreement is plausible.

\subsection*{Quantum Corrections Commentary}
Gauge couplings emerge from statistical properties of filaments; quantum corrections would manifest through scale-dependence of filament tension and rigidity. Although not explicitly derived, this is a plausible mechanism.

\subsection*{Group-Theoretical Plausibility}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Winding number (π₁) corresponds naturally to \(U(1)\).
    \item Linking number (second homotopy group) plausibly associated with \(SU(2)\).
    \item Triple linking and higher entanglement correspond to higher non-Abelian groups like \(SU(3)\).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Failure Mode and Falsifiability}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Disagreement between predicted and measured gauge coupling ratios.
    \item Inability to account for renormalization group running.
    \item Filament parameters deviating from expected values at high or low energies.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Critical Commentary}
\textbf{Strengths}:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Derivations are dimensionally consistent.
    \item Falsifiable predictions are made.
    \item Group-theoretical mapping is plausible and physically motivated.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Weaknesses}:
\begin{itemize}
    \item No explicit treatment of quantum corrections.
    \item Boundary conditions and stability not deeply analyzed.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Recommendations}
Pursue explicit quantum correction derivations and investigate stability under filament perturbations.
